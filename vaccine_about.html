Mapping the New Politics of Care: Inequality and Vaccine Redistribution Within Phase 1a

All people in the United States have been granted the right to have access to free, safe, and effective COVID-19 vaccines. But due to the limited doses available, states have enacted policies prioritizing specific groups based on reasonable—but subjective—weighing of scientific, practical, and ethical evidence and values.

The goal of this map is to examine how prioritizing different groups for vaccination shifts the terrain of vaccine allocation and impacts which communities are protected first.
